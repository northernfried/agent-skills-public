# Strategy Skills

Use this folder for product leadership strategy work that shapes yearly direction before portfolio planning, refinement, and delivery execution.

## Purpose

These skills help product leadership:

- Define the organization's products, features, and marketed solutions in one place.
- Define yearly product goals and measurable quarterly objectives.
- Create a clear product strategy for where to play, what to solve, and what to defer.
- Focus investment on important verticals, business topics, and commercial opportunities.
- Align the investment portfolio to product initiatives and delivery expectations.
- Evaluate quarterly whether strategy is on track, drifting, or needs correction.
- Clarify why the organization should invest, what success looks like, and what client experience should become.

## Skills Index

- `product-and-solution-portfolio-definition.md`
  - Defines products, their features, and the client-facing solutions being marketed.
  - Flags duplicate solutions, product overlap, and required justification for overlap.

- `annual-goals-and-quarterly-objectives.md`
  - Defines annual goals and measurable quarterly checkpoints.
  - Establishes review triggers and adjustment cadence across the year.

- `product-strategy-and-business-focus.md`
  - Builds yearly product strategy around verticals, business topics, timelines, campaigns, and commercial opportunities.
  - Answers why to invest, what success looks like, and what client experience should become.

- `investment-portfolio-alignment.md`
  - Aligns strategy themes to initiatives and investment areas.
  - Highlights essential versus optional investments and portfolio gaps.

- `quarterly-strategy-evaluation-and-adjustment.md`
  - Reviews quarterly whether strategy is on track, drifting, or needs reset.
  - Recommends leadership adjustments based on outcomes and changed assumptions.

## Suggested Usage Order

1. Start with `product-and-solution-portfolio-definition.md` to define products, solutions, overlap boundaries, and offering rationale.
2. Use `annual-goals-and-quarterly-objectives.md` to define year intent and quarterly measures.
3. Use `product-strategy-and-business-focus.md` to determine where to play, what to solve, and what critical business topics must be supported.
4. Use `investment-portfolio-alignment.md` to connect the strategy to initiatives and investment decisions.
5. Use `quarterly-strategy-evaluation-and-adjustment.md` each quarter to test whether strategy is being delivered or needs correction.

## Inputs To Gather

- Current product catalog, feature map, and marketed solutions.
- Known overlap areas, duplicate offerings, and unclear positioning.
- Annual business targets and strategic constraints.
- Product portfolio scope and strategic ambitions.
- Important verticals, segments, regions, and growth opportunities.
- Critical yearly campaigns, deadlines, obligations, and market windows.
- Candidate initiatives, investment capacity, and delivery constraints.
- Current performance baseline and customer experience pain points.

## Output Expectations

- A clear map of products, features, and marketed solutions.
- Clear justification for any product overlap and no duplicated solutions.
- A yearly product strategy with selective focus areas.
- Annual goals and quarterly measurable objectives.
- Investment rationale and aligned portfolio themes.
- Explicit view of what success and client experience should look like.
- Quarterly review outputs showing whether strategy is on track or needs adjustment.
